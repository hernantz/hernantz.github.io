All hail the textual communication overlord

Published on Fri 29 April 2016, under Programming, tagged with ideas.

Problems¶

Too many apps. Slack, irc, sms, rss, comments, forums, etc.

Email feels odd for realtime communication.

Not easy to create lists.

Nested conversations and quoting should be collapsed.

Everthing is an email¶

UI problem, default subject like chat with Sara and Sara will see chat with Frank or a group that can be named.

But every email that has a subject set is a new conversation. E.g: vacations planning.

Remember to support both formats: text and html (emojis, gifs, embedded videos)

News sites and forums should converge to mailing lists, where every topic or article is an email, and replies are comments. This would also replace RSS.

We still need to introduce the concept of channels. Channels are groups of groups. This allows you to further classify messages under a shared context (subforums?).

Any message is an email and can be labeled.

What about inline comments in other apps? like photo albums or google docs? Well for every piece of content that can be commented, there should be a list. mailto: ?

Problems not solved by email: file sharing, voice chat or videoconferencing for that matter. This should be solved by other programs, and you would potentially share a link to that app, as an email of course.

Passwords are emails¶

You can create a new password everytime, and get it emailed.
